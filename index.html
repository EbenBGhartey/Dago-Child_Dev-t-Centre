    About Us

    Welcome to Dago Methodist Child Development Centre

    Know more about us, Partner us and get exciting with us.

    Dago Methodist Child Development Centre is a non governmental organization (NGO) owned by Dago Wesley Methodist Church in partnership with compassion international Ghana. We are aimed at releasing children from poverty, providing better education for children and ensuring a healthy life style of children under our care.
    The project was established on 1st September, 2009 with 250 registered beneficiaries. Currently, the total number of registered beneficiaries stands at 308.

    Curriculums We Offer

    Dago Methodist Child Development Centre use HOLISTIC DEVELOPMENT approach to train beneficiaries
    In our field of studies, the following curriculums are used.

    Spiritual Development: Demonstrate commitment to the Lordship of Christ.
    Helps to know and understand the bible.

    Spiritual activities include bible studies, retreat, bible quiz, preaching, devotions, bible stories, donation of bible, camps, discipleship training, evangelism.

    Socio-Emotional Development: We want the children and youth to interact with each other in a healthy and compassionate manner.

    Socio Emotional Activities include; Educate beneficiaries on Conflict Management, Managing emotions, organize choreography, organize games, educate beneficiaries on public speaking.

    Cognitive Development: Empower beneficiaries to complete basic school and also demonstrate commitment to learn and utilize at least one income generating activities.

    Cognitive Activities include; Organize literacy education, career guidance, extra tuition, supply educational supplies, organize inter schools quiz competition, support payment of fees. We provide educational support from Basic school level to Tertiary level.

    Physical Development: Demonstrate approporiate understanding of his or her physical body. Make wise life choices with health and sexuality.

    Physical Activities include; Health education on Malaria, URTI, Handwashing, reproductive health,Environmental cleanliness, HIV AIDs, Teen age pregnancy, health screening, pay medical biils.

    Things We Do

    We engage our children in the following activities to help them acquire the neccessary skills,
    and also build them up with a life career and a brighter future.

    • Bible Studies & Spiritual Formation

      Beneficiaries are engaged in bible studies and also encouraged to participate in various church activities.

    • Youth Guidance & monitoring Program

      Educative programs are organized for the youth to become agents of change in their schools families and communties.

    • My Life & Carreer Guidance

      Beneficiaries are assigned to mentors for assistance in choosing and persuing the right career paths.

    • Educational Programs

      We enroll school drop-outs and children of school going-age who are not in school to school. Literacy classes are organized for them to learn to read and write.Educational materials are also supplied to them.

    • Sports & Games

      The organization participates in sports activities which is also as part of the physical development.

    • Donation to the Aged

      Beneficiaries occasionally visit the old age in the society to offer them some support by offering them food and also fetching water for them.

    • Outreach Programmes

      Through local resource mobilization and strategic alliance with other partners,

    • Volunteering

      We are committed to achieve maximum results through Volunteering. We train children and youth to give back to the community.

    Questions You May Ask
    How do Dago Methodist Child Development Centre Select Beneficiaries?

    Dago Methodist Child Development Centre select the neediest in the community. The poorest of the poor. We use poverty probability index for the selection of our beneficiaries..

    Do Dago Methodist Child Development Centre only support beneficiaries in education?

    At Dago Methodist Child Development Centre, the 309 beneficiaries who are selected are the core beneficiaries who receive full and regular support in education. However there are other non beneficiaries who also receive regular support through external funding , strategic alliance and local resource mobilization. We supported 6 students to study in Arizona State University,USA through full scholarship. We curently support a medical student from University of Cape Coast Medical school and a Law student also in University of Cape Coast...

    Do we,(DCDC) take all responsibility concerning beneficiaries from parents?

    We do not make parents irresponsible. We rather empower them to protect their children and provide needed support. We organise parenting education, child protection education, entrepreneurship education for parents. We also organise father's and mother's day celebrations to help them achieve this goal.

    Do Dago Methodist Child Development Centre help non beneficiaries?

    In most cases, no. Such direct aid can lead to misuse of funds, children's unhealthy dependency on an outside organization, and difficulty in monitoring individual development goals. Compassion funds church-based programs that provide developmental opportunities and benefits agreed upon by the church and Compassion. The standard is that funds are used on meaningful developmental opportunities for the program's children.

    How do Dago Methodist Child Development Centre fund the project?

    From statistics to stories, there is growing evidence that our program gives children a strong opportunity to overcome poverty.

    Can I get support from Dago Methodist Child Development Centre if I don't come from Dago?

    Dago Methodist Child Development Centre through locsl resource mobilization and strategic alliance with other partners reach out to other communities in Ekumfi District and Mfantseman municipality.We are ready to help the neediest irrespective of the location.
